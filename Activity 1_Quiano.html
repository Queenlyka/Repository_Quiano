Adventures in Code-Fu


My HTML Journey from Chaos to Cat Videos


Or, how I accidentally created a self-playing cat videos website (and almost lost my sanity)


Armed with boundless enthusiasm and zero coding experience, I embarked on my Quest to Conquer HTML .

Little did I know my journey would be less "Lord of the Rings" and more "Three Stooges."

My first Website lovingly named "Brenda's Blossoming Boutique", resembled a digital Jackson Pollock painting.

Tags were scattered like colors clashed like a sunset on Mars and the navigation system could only be described as "whimsical"

(by a very generous browser.)

Determined to redeem myself, I tackled a "simple" project: a cat video gallery. When ensued was a hilarious (and slightly terrifying) ballet misplaced semicolons, rogue line breaks, and images that danced uncontrollably across the screen. At one point, my entire website transformed into an auto-playing cat video slideshow, much to the amusement (and slight concern) of my family.


After several meldowns, countless tutorials, and gallons of coffee. I finally manage to wrangle my code into submission.

My cat video gallery wasn't exactly "elegant", but it functioned (mostly) And hey, i learned that even the clumsiest coder can create something... interesting. So, here's to Brenda's Blossoming Boutique, the self-playing cat videos extravaganza, and the never-ending quest to master the art of HTML!
